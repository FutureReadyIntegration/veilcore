# Veil OS Audit Log Retention & Protection Policy

**Audience:** Security, Compliance, Maintainers, Auditors  
**Control Type:** Technical / Administrative  
**Last Updated:** 2026-01-26

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how audit logs generated by the Veil OS Organ Engine
are protected, retained, and reviewed.

Audit logs are considered **security records**.

---

## 2. Audit Log Scope

The Veil OS CLI produces audit records for:
- All command executions
- All dry-run and real-write actions
- All failures and exceptions
- All targets affected

Audit logs are written in **append-only JSON Lines format**.

---

## 3. Log Locations

Audit logs are written to the first writable location:

1. `/var/log/veil_os/veil_audit.jsonl` (preferred)
2. `/opt/veil_os/logs/veil_audit.jsonl`
3. `/tmp/veil_os_logs/veil_audit.jsonl` (fallback only)

---

## 4. Retention Requirements

### Default Retention
- **Minimum retention:** 180 days
- **Recommended retention:** 365 days
- **Maximum retention:** governed by organizational policy

### Rotation
- Logs are rotated by size
- Rotation does not delete logs until retention requirements are met

---

## 5. Access Controls

Audit logs MUST:
- Be writable only by the runtime service user
- Be readable only by authorized operators or auditors
- NOT be modified or truncated outside of rotation

Recommended permissions:
```bash
chmod 750 /var/log/veil_os
chmod 640 /var/log/veil_os/veil_audit.jsonl
